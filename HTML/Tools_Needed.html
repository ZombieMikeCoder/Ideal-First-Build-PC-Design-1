Tools Needed

Before starting to build a PC there are several basic tools that will be needed to install all of the hardware together. I have listed below the main tools best to have, you can also now buy many different kits that include all of these main tools along with extra tools that can come in very handy.


Anti-Static Wrist Band

A must have when handling static sensitive hardware such as the motherboard. These will help to disperse static electricity that may build up. Anti-static wrist straps are probably the most convenient and cost effective way of protecting your new hardware.



Assorted Screwdrivers

These are the most useful tools in PC building/repair since the majority of fixings in a PC case are secured by screws. The 2 main types are Philips and flat head although you may find some types like star head (Torx) screws, itâ€™s normally best to have a selection of bits before starting any project.



Cable Ties

Where necessary, keep things tidy by carefully tying back loose cabling inside the PC Case. This will also help airflow around the machine. Be sure to use plastic ties only.




Small Wire Cutters

Useful for an all manner of things, wire cutters are particularly handy when using cable ties to neatly clip off the exces. These cutters are sometimes known as side cutters. These can also be very usefull if you want to make customer wiring you get nice clean cuts to the wire each time.



Long-Nose Pliers

When fingers are just too big for the job, these will help plugging and unplugging connectors in the very hard to reach areas. Long-nose pliers are great when dealing with old IDE configuration jumpers.




Small Torch

It can get dark inside a PC Case once everything is fitted. This should throw some light on the subject. You will find the smaller the torch the easier it will be to fit into small spaces allowing you to see dark corners more easier.



Pencil and Paper

It's always best to have a pencil and I would recommend having a small notes book with you when building your PC rather than just scraps of paper. This is a quick and easy way of jotting down any important notes for later reference such as what case fan is linked to the motherboard, etc.



Magnetic Tray

Magnetic Trays help to keep small screws in one place when installing components, they also prevent lossing any screws within the installation phase. These are relatively cheap and can save you a lot of time searching for dropped screws, you can also drop any multi-screwdriver ends into these to save losing them.
